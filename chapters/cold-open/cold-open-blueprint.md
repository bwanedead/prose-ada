## Purpose
Create a short, diegetic opening fragment that:
- sets mood and intrigue without explaining
- primes the reader for history/myth as “data”
- becomes retroactively meaningful after the finale
- closes a loop: the fragment is a recovered shard of Ada’s end-of-book sign-off message

---

## Format
- Presented as an **artifact** (transcript excerpt / recovered log fragment / partial printout / redacted message).
- Includes **minimal metadata** (just enough to feel real, not enough to explain).
- Length target: **1 paragraph** (2 max).

---

## Required Layers (Double Meaning)
### Layer 1 — Surface Read (first pass)
Reads as a grounded warning or reflection about:
- history
- records
- distortion
- what survives vs what is true

### Layer 2 — Deep Read (reread)
Hints that:
- “history” is plural
- lineage is branching
- the observer is inside the structure
- what we call history may be only one surviving version

### Layer 3 — Method (tone anchor)
Contains an epistemic directive aligned with the story ethos:
- triangulate evidence
- don’t trust single sources
- patterns across domains are higher signal than isolated claims

---

## Constraints (Anti-Cringe)
- Not authorial. Must be plausibly in-world.
- No manifesto tone.
- No mystical certainty.
- No explicit mention of “timelines,” “multiverse,” or “convergence.”
- Short, calm, high-implication.

---

## Loop Integrity Contract (Cold Open ↔ Finale)
- The cold open is a **partial recovery** of the sign-off created in the finale (see `Finale Blueprint — Book One`).
- The finale will show Ada initiating the sign-off, but the reader will **not** see/hear the full message.
- This fragment must remain plausible as a shard that could survive distribution, corruption, encryption, or redaction.

---

## Signature Requirement
Include **one subtle signature cue** that will later match Ada’s end-state voice or the sign-off medium:
- a phrase cadence
- a formatting convention
- a metadata pattern
- a recurring conceptual move

This cue should not call attention to itself.

---

## Resolution Outcome
On first read, the cold open feels like a mysterious but grounded artifact.  
On reread, it feels like an inevitable piece of the ending.
